**Question**: What is historical change at the constraint boundary?

## The Constraint Set

Historical systems face evolving constraints:

1. **Geography**: Physical environment possibilities
2. **Technology**: What's possible to build/do
3. **Population**: Demographics and scale
4. **Resources**: Available materials and energy
5. **Knowledge**: What's understood
6. **Social structure**: How people organize
7. **Ideas**: What's thinkable
8. **Power**: Who can impose will

## Core Hypothesis

**Historical change happens when constraint boundaries shift.**

Not "great men shape history" or "material forces determine everything."
But: "History is boundary navigation as constraints shift."

## Key Insights

### 1. Revolutions at Boundary Collapse (10/10)

Why do revolutions happen?

**When current boundary position becomes unstable.**

French Revolution: Old regime couldn't satisfy constraint set (fiscal + legitimacy + popular expectation)
Industrial Revolution: New technology created new boundaries
Information Revolution: New boundaries in knowledge/communication

**Revolutions mark boundary phase transitions.**

### 2. Longue Durée as Slow Boundary Drift (9/10)

Braudel's longue durée: Slow, deep historical change.

**This is slow constraint boundary evolution.**

- Climate change shifts agricultural boundaries
- Population growth shifts resource boundaries
- Technology accumulation shifts capability boundaries

**History operates on multiple timescales of boundary change.**

### 3. Historical Contingency as Boundary Sensitivity (9/10)

Why do small events sometimes matter? (Chaos, path dependence)

**Near boundaries, small perturbations can trigger large shifts.**

Far from boundary: Small changes, small effects
Near boundary: Small changes, potentially large effects

**Historical contingency is boundary sensitivity.**

### 4. Rise and Fall of Empires (9/10)

Why do empires rise and fall?

**They find and then lose boundary positions.**

Rise: Find stable boundary (territory, resources, organization)
Stability: Maintain boundary position
Fall: Can't adapt when constraints shift

**Imperial history is boundary occupation and displacement.**

### 5. Historical Inevitability vs. Contingency (8/10)

Debate: Was historical outcome inevitable or contingent?

**Boundary resolution**: The BOUNDARY was inevitable (given constraints). The exact POSITION was contingent.

Industrial revolution was inevitable (constraint pressure).
England first was contingent (specific boundary position).

### 6. Historical Progress? (8/10)

Is history progressive?

**Some boundaries expand (knowledge, capability).**
**Some boundaries cycle (political, social).**

Progress = expanding boundaries in certain dimensions.
Not: Linear march to better.
But: Irregular boundary expansion.
