## The Constraint Set

Communication systems face constraints:

1. **Expression**: Must encode thought
2. **Comprehension**: Must be decoded
3. **Efficiency**: Limited bandwidth (speech, writing)
4. **Precision**: Must distinguish meanings
5. **Learnability**: Must be acquirable
6. **Flexibility**: Must handle novel thoughts

## Core Hypothesis

**Language is shared structure at the expression-comprehension boundary.**

Not "system of signs" but "the boundary where private thought becomes public meaning."

### 1. Language as Boundary Technology (10/10)

What is language really?

**Technology for crossing the mind-mind boundary.**

Thoughts are private. Language makes them shareable.
But: Translation is lossy. Perfect expression is impossible.

**Language IS the boundary phenomenon between minds.**

### 2. Meaning at Interpretation Boundary (10/10)

Where is meaning?

**At the boundary between expression and interpretation.**

Not in words (symbols are arbitrary)
Not in heads (thoughts are private)
At the boundary where expression meets interpretation

**Meaning is boundary phenomenon, not located substance.**

### 3. Grammar as Boundary Constraint (9/10)

Why does grammar exist?

**Grammar constrains expression to enable comprehension.**

Infinite possible expressions → too hard to decode
Grammar limits expression → makes decoding tractable

**Grammar is the constraint that creates the language boundary.**

When do new words/constructions emerge?

**When new boundaries need crossing.**

Technology creates new concepts → new words
New experiences → new expressions
New social configurations → new speech patterns

**Language evolves as boundaries shift.**

**Poetry is meta-boundary exploration.**

**Because language IS the boundary, not a bridge across it.**

**Communication is not transmission. It's coordinated boundary approach.**
