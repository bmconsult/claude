## Boundary Analysis: Where to Position

### Current Market Landscape

```
                        HIGH CAPITAL REQUIRED

    ($$$, time-heavy)  ($$$, capital-heavy)

LOW TIME <---------------+---------------> HIGH TIME
                               |
    Gig/Freelance              |     Employment
    (low $, flexible)          |     ($, time-traded)
                               |
                               v
                        LOW CAPITAL REQUIRED
```

### The Unoccupied Position

Most approaches cluster at vertices:
- Consulting: High time, high expertise, medium income
- Products: High capital, high leverage, variable income
- Employment: High time, low autonomy, stable income
- Gig work: Low everything

**The boundary opportunity**:

```
CONSTRAINT-BOUNDARY CONSULTING
Position: Medium time + Low capital + High expertise leverage + Recurring revenue
```

This is the B2B2C model:
- Don't sell to end clients (high time per deal)
- Don't build products (high capital)
- Sell to other consultants (leverage their time)
- Recurring subscription (income stability)
- Unique method (can't be commoditized)

## Why This Position Is Stable

### It satisfies multiple constraints simultaneously:

| Constraint | How Satisfied |
|------------|--------------|
| Income | $15-20k/month from 5-7 subscription clients |
| Time | Scalable - method delivery, not hours |
| Capital | Low - documentation + calls + analysis |
| Skill | Leverages synthesis/insight capability |
| Network | B2B is smaller network than B2C |
| Energy | Varied work (writing, calls, analysis) |
| Autonomy | Full control over methods and schedule |
| Meaning | Genuinely novel insights, helping others |

### It's at the boundary because:

- More clients → More time → hits time constraint
- Less clients → Less income → hits income constraint
- Different model (products) → needs capital → hits capital constraint
- Different model (employment) → loses autonomy → hits autonomy constraint

**5-7 clients is the boundary sweet spot.**
