**Question**: What is strategic equilibrium at the constraint boundary?

## The Constraint Set

Game-theoretic agents face constraints that cannot all be maximized:

1. **Payoff constraint**: Maximize own utility
2. **Information constraint**: Act with incomplete/asymmetric info
3. **Commitment constraint**: Can/cannot bind future actions
4. **Rationality constraint**: Bounded computation, bounded foresight
5. **Time constraint**: Finite horizon vs. infinite
6. **Coordination constraint**: Alignment with other agents

Impossible trade-offs:
- Perfect rationality requires infinite computation (bounded)
- Full commitment removes flexibility (sub-optimal in changing games)
- Maximum individual payoff often destroys cooperative surplus
- Perfect information eliminates strategic advantage

## Core Hypothesis

**Equilibrium concepts are stable positions at the boundary of strategic possibility.**

Nash equilibrium: Boundary where no unilateral improvement exists
Pareto optimal: Boundary where no one improves without someone losing
Evolutionarily stable: Boundary where no mutant strategy invades

**Equilibria ARE boundaries.**

### 4. Evolution of Cooperation (9/10)

The problem: Individual rationality → defection. Collective optimum → cooperation.

**Traditional view**: How do we overcome the temptation to defect?

**Boundary view**: Cooperation exists at the boundary where individual and collective constraints intersect.

Mechanisms that work:
- Reputation (information boundary shift)
- Punishment (payoff boundary shift)
- Repeated games (time constraint shift)
- Kin selection (payoff redefinition)

**All mechanisms work by moving agents TO a cooperation-supporting boundary.**

**Real agents exist at the boundary of rationality.**

Behavioral economics findings are NOT "deviations from rationality."

- Loss aversion: Risk management at uncertainty boundary
- Hyperbolic discounting: Time management at planning boundary

**"Biases" are boundary phenomena.**
