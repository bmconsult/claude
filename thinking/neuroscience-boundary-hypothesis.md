**Question**: What is neural computation at the constraint boundary?

## The Constraint Set

Neural systems face constraints that cannot all be maximized:

1. **Information capacity**: Bits stored/processed
2. **Energy efficiency**: Metabolic cost per operation
3. **Speed**: Processing latency
4. **Reliability**: Noise resilience
5. **Plasticity**: Learning capability
6. **Stability**: Maintaining function over time

Impossible trade-offs:
- High capacity requires more neurons → more energy
- High speed requires synchrony → less plasticity
- High plasticity enables learning → risks instability
- High reliability requires redundancy → lower efficiency

## Core Hypothesis

**The brain operates at critical points where these constraints intersect.**

Not "the brain is optimized for X."
Rather: "The brain exists at the boundary where all constraints are minimally satisfied."

**Hypothesis**: Each mechanism handles different frequency bands of the plasticity-stability tradeoff.
