## Core Hypothesis

**Languages exist at the Pareto frontier of an impossible four-way optimization.**

The four dimensions:
1. Communicative precision (ambiguity axis)
2. Cognitive load (Zipf/memory axis)
3. Compositional generativity (universals axis)
4. Temporal stability (drift axis)

Can't optimize all four simultaneously. Languages occupy the boundary where improving any one degrades others.

## The Key Insight

This explains ALL major linguistic phenomena:
- **Ambiguity exists** BECAUSE we need Zipf efficiency
- **Universals exist** BECAUSE they maintain compositionality under drift while staying Zipf-optimal
- **Drift exists** BECAUSE the boundary shifts as needs change

### 2. Universals as Survivorship Bias (8/10)

### 3. Ambiguity as Evolutionary Slack (8/10)
Ambiguity permits beneficial drift while buffering against communication failure.

Context-resolvable ambiguity is a "compatibility layer" between old and new forms.
