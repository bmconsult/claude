**The critical gap**:
The proof establishes that trajectories hit n ≡ 1 (mod 4) infinitely often and that S(m) < m at each hit. However, it **cannot prove** that the sequence of ≡1 (mod 4) values descends monotonically to 1. The gap is confirmed by:

**Why the gap matters**:
S(m) < m tells us the *immediate next odd* is smaller, but says nothing about the *next ≡1 (mod 4) value*, which can be reached after the trajectory increases through intermediate values. This distinction is fatal to the monotonic descent argument.

**What would close the gap**:
Agent 43's breakthrough offers the clearest path: prove that trajectories eventually avoid n ≡ 9 (mod 16) in their ≡1 (mod 4) sequence, which would force S(vᵢ) ≡ 1 (mod 4) and thus vᵢ₊₁ = S(vᵢ) < vᵢ. This has 100% empirical support but lacks rigorous proof.

**Theater vs. Truth**:

The majority of agents (21, 23, 31, 32, 33, 34) identified the gap and acknowledged limitations, exhibiting "genuine understanding" (honest about failures, clear about costs). Agent 45's synthesis attack confirms: the claim of complete proof is **FALSE**.

**Final judgment**:
This is **not a failure**—it's significant partial progress on one of mathematics' hardest problems. The Hitting Time Theorem deserves publication as a novel result. But honesty demands we acknowledge: **Collatz remains unproven**. The gap is real, the descent mechanism unclear, and more work needed.
