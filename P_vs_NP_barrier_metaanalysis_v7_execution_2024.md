## Final Synthesis: Answer to "P = NP?"

### Direct Answer
**We do not know.** The question remains open after 50+ years.

### Current Consensus
~83% of experts believe **P ≠ NP** based on intuition that verification is fundamentally easier than solving. However, this is not a proof.

### Why It's Hard
Three barriers block all standard proof techniques:
1. **Relativization** (Baker-Gill-Solovay 1975): Techniques that work in all oracle worlds fail
2. **Natural Proofs** (Razborov-Rudich 1997): Constructive lower bounds likely impossible
3. **Algebrization** (Aaronson-Wigderson 2009): Algebraic techniques fail

Any proof must transcend ALL THREE simultaneously. No known technique does this.

### Approaches Analyzed
After systematic analysis via 177-agent adversarial architecture:

**Best approach identified:** Meta-theoretical barrier formalization
**Confidence it resolves P vs NP:** 5% [1%, 15%]
**Confidence it generates insights:** 60% [40%, 75%]

**Why low confidence:**
- Historical precedent against meta-approaches (Gödel's limits, CH independence)
- Epistemology vs ontology confusion (studies our failures, not the truth)
- Critical dependence on unverified assumptions (gaps must exist and be exploitable)
- Base rate: novel approaches to P vs NP almost never succeed
