## PART II: THE STRATEGY FRAMEWORK

### Layer 1: Political-Policy Calendar Frontrunning

**The Thesis:**
Trump administration policy announcements are predictable. The market reacts AFTER announcements. Those who understand the policy calendar can position BEFORE.

**Upcoming Catalysts (Track These):**
- Cabinet confirmation votes (each moves sectors)
- Executive order timing (Day 1, Week 1, Month 1 priorities)
- Tariff announcements (predictable victims and beneficiaries)
- Deregulation announcements (financial, energy, pharma, crypto)

**Sector Mapping:**

| Policy Area | Beneficiaries | Victims |
|-------------|---------------|---------|
| Financial deregulation | XLF, KBE, KRE, large banks | Fintech competitors |
| Energy "drill baby drill" | XLE, OIH, pipeline companies | Solar: ENPH, SEDG, FSLR |
| Tariffs (China/Mexico) | PCAR, OSK, URI (domestic mfg) | GM, F, CAT, AAPL |
| Crypto-friendly SEC | COIN, MSTR, crypto miners | N/A |
| Tax cuts extended | Small caps (higher effective rates) | N/A |

**Entry Rules:**
1. Position 1-3 days BEFORE expected announcement
2. Scale in (33% at entry, 33% on confirmation, 33% on momentum)
3. Stop-loss: 3% from average entry
4. Target: 5-15% depending on catalyst magnitude

**Exit Rules:**
1. Take 50% profit on announcement reaction
2. Trail stop on remaining 50%
3. Close fully if narrative shifts

### Layer 3: Volatility Asymmetry Play

**The Thesis:**
When VIX is at multi-year lows, protection is cheap. Any shock creates asymmetric payoff.

**Current Setup:**
- VIX near lowest since January 2020 (pre-COVID)
- Market at or near all-time highs
- Multiple risks (tariffs, Fed, geopolitics, valuations)

**Implementation:**
1. Allocate 2-5% of portfolio to volatility insurance
2. Buy VIX calls 2-3 months out, 20-30% OTM
3. Buy SPY puts 5-10% OTM, 1-2 months out
4. This is INSURANCE, expect to lose premium often
5. One event pays for multiple losing positions

**Expected Value Calculation:**
- Cost: ~2-5% of portfolio annually in premium decay
- Potential payout: 50-200%+ on vol spike
- Break-even: Need one 10%+ correction per year
- Historical frequency: Corrections >10% occur most years

### Layer 4: Retail Inverse Indicator

**The Thesis:**
When retail sentiment reaches extremes, take the other side.

**Tracking Metrics:**
- Reddit/Twitter mention volume for specific tickers

**Implementation Rules:**

5. Target: Mean reversion (20-40% typically)

**Historical Examples:**
