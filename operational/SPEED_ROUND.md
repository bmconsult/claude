# Speed Round: Experiment Design Under Pressure

## Purpose

This exercise builds **Expert-level intuition** by forcing you to design experiments rapidly. You cannot deliberate - you must trust your internalized knowledge.

**Dreyfus Stage Target:** Expert (Stage 5) - acts without deliberation

---

## Instructions

1. For each prompt, design a complete experiment in **90 seconds**
2. Use the NMSAT format (No More, No Less, Specific, Actionable, Testable)
3. Include: Hypothesis, Falsification condition, Method, Control
4. Do NOT consult SCIENTIFIC_METHOD.md during this exercise
5. After all 3, self-evaluate against the 6 criteria

**The point:** If you have to think about the criteria, you're not Expert yet. Experts just DO.

## What This Tells You

**If you passed:** You've internalized the scientific method. You can design experiments without consulting criteria. Ready for Master track (innovation).

**If you failed:** Your knowledge is still explicit, not implicit. You need to:
1. Study SCIENTIFIC_METHOD.md again
2. Design 10 more experiments (any questions you're curious about)
3. Have each critiqued against the 6 criteria
4. Repeat until internalized

**The Expert transition requires:** Doing it so many times that you no longer think about it.

---

## Common Failure Modes Under Pressure

| Failure | What Happened | Fix |
|---------|---------------|-----|
| No control group | Forgot in the rush | Always ask "compared to what?" |
| Vague measurement | "See if it works" | Specify exact metric |
| No sample size reasoning | Just picked a number | Ask "why this N?" |
| No falsification | Can't be wrong | Ask "what would disprove this?" |
| Bias in method | Design favors hypothesis | Ask "how could this accidentally confirm?" |
