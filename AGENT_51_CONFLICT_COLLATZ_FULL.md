### T4: Forward vs Backward Analysis
**The Situation**:
- Forward: Track trajectories n→1 (dynamical systems)
- Backward: Analyze reverse tree 1→n (combinatorics)
- Different proof architectures, different insights

**What It Generates**:

1. **Dual Perspectives**:
   - Forward reveals: descent bias, compression dynamics, stopping time behavior
   - Backward reveals: tree structure, coverage patterns, branching logic

2. **Complementary Tools**:
   - Forward: ergodic theory, Markov chains, measure theory
   - Backward: graph theory, combinatorics, induction

3. **Synthetic Possibilities**:
   - Backward tree might prove structural coverage
   - Forward dynamics might prove quantitative descent
   - Together: complete argument

**Why Productive**: Each perspective sees what the other misses. Forward sees "why it descends," backward sees "what it must cover."

**How to Use**: Don't pick sides. Use backward analysis to establish structural constraints, forward analysis to prove dynamics satisfy those constraints.
